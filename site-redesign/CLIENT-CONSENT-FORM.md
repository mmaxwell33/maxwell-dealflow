# Client Consent to Feature a Property — draft templates

**Purpose:** the board's compliance analyst (Abena) confirmed you may not publish a client's home address + sale price on your website without their **written** consent — a binding Canadian privacy ruling (PIPEDA Case #2009-002) is directly on point. These two short templates get you that consent. Send whichever fits the deal.

> ⚠️ **Before you use these:** run them past your eXp broker / compliance contact once. They're a solid plain-English starting point built on PIPEDA consent principles, not a lawyer-reviewed final form. eXp may have its own required wording.

The point of sending these this week is the **free test the board recommended**: email your 2–3 past clients, and see how many come back signed *with permission to use your own photos*. That result decides whether the website's "Sold" section is worth building — before anyone writes a line of code.

---

## Version A — Buyer-side deal ("I represented the buyer")

> **Subject: Quick permission to feature your home purchase on my website**
>
> Hi [Client first name],
>
> I'm refreshing my website and I'd love to include the home you bought as one of the client stories I'm proud of — framed as *"Represented the buyer."* Before I put anything up, I need your written OK, because it would show details about your home.
>
> Could you reply **"I consent"** to the below (or reply with any changes)?
>
> ---
>
> **I, [Client full name], give Maxwell Midodzi (REALTOR®, eXp Realty) permission to publish the following about the property at [address], which I purchased with his help, on his website and social media:**
>
> - [ ] The **neighbourhood/town only** (e.g. "Mount Pearl") — *or* — [ ] the **full street address**
> - [ ] The **sale price** — *or* — [ ] **no price shown**
> - [ ] **Photos that Maxwell took himself** (not the MLS listing photos)
> - [ ] A short description of the outcome (e.g. "first-time buyer, closed in 34 days"), with his role stated as *"Represented the buyer."*
>
> I understand this is voluntary, and I can ask him to **remove it at any time** by emailing Maxwell.Midodzi@exprealty.com.
>
> Name: ______________________  Signature: ______________________  Date: __________

## The rules these forms enforce (from the compliance brief)

1. **No publish without written consent** — the checkbox/signature is the record. Keep the signed reply.
2. **Role must be accurate** — "Represented the buyer" for buyer-side; never imply you listed a home you didn't.
3. **Your own photos only** on buyer-side deals — the MLS listing photos belong to the other brokerage/photographer; you can't republish them without written licence.
4. **Right to withdraw** — always offer removal on request (PIPEDA requirement).
5. Every published card also carries **"Brokered by eXp Realty"** and the CREA trademark line (already built into the site footer).

## What to do with the results
- **≥1 signed with own-photo rights** → the Sold section is worth building (Phase 2 in board report № 2).
- **Zero come back** → skip the Sold section for now; the site already has the honest credibility strip + free guides doing that job. You lost nothing but one email.
